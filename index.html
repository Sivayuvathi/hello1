QUANTUM COMPUTING

Quantum computing is a field of computer science that uses quantum mechanics to solve complex problems faster than traditional computers

Quantum computers use specialized hardware to perform calculations that are based on the principles of quantum physics, such as superposition, entanglement, and quantum interference

Quantum computing involves qubits.

Unlike a normal computer bit, which can be either 0 or 1, a qubit can exist in a multidimensional state.

The power of quantum computers grows exponentially with more qubits.

Classical computers that add more bits can increase power only linearly.
